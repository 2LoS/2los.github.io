The First Dawn

It’s 5:30am. The first shades of light are appearing over the horizon. Like purple and orange waves, they pass through a clauster of milky clouds. They kindly snorkel on the waves of light, in the ocean of the sky. The songs of birds fly beyond the dark woods, now sailing and then descending. The autumn flavors disappear in the fresh breeze: it tastes of wet flowers, of lovely greetings. It is even more interesting when the cup of coffee flows some hints in the air: an unique, exotic parfum. The night is slowly disappearing in a warm, comforting hug.

The early morning is a special time, when the quiet atmosphere and the fresh air allows for a moment of reflection, after the silent darkness of the night, before the excited lightness of the day. In such a quiet and regenerative span, the thoughts can fluently move, freely swim in the mind.
